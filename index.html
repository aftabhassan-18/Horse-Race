Credit outruns trade in financial crisis transmission

Pre-crisis credit exposure consistently predicts deeper GDP contractions during the Global Financial Crisis. Trade openness, run through the same models, never does.

Hassan Aftab, Pitzer College
130 economies, 2000–2019
5 specifications tested
-0.48pp growth lost per 1 s.d. rise in credit exposure
+12.6pp rise in crisis probability per unit credit-to-GDP

The credit channel survives every specification. Trade never shows up.

Panel data on 130 economies, with pre-crisis credit and trade exposure fixed at 2006 values to avoid contamination from the crisis itself. The credit channel wins the horse race in all five specifications; advanced economies enter the GFC with roughly four times the credit exposure of developing economies (106.9% vs. 25.6% of GDP), so resilience frameworks built around trade exposure are guarding the wrong flank.
